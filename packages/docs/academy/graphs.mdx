In the previous module, you learned that a World is a graph. This lesson
explains how to build that graph.

## The triple: The atomic unit of knowledge

Every piece of information in a World is stored as a **triple**. A triple
consists of three components:

1.  **Subject**: The entity you are describing, for example, "Ethan".
2.  **Predicate**: The relationship or property, for example, "worksAt".
3.  **Object**: The value or another entity related to the subject, for example,
    "Wazoo".

Together, they form a clear semantic statement: **Ethan works at Wazoo.**

### Graph formation

Triples are powerful because they are universal. You can express everything from
simple facts to complex hierarchies through this structure.

As you add multiple triples, they naturally form a **Graph**:

- "Ethan worksAt Wazoo"
- "Wazoo locatedIn Seattle"
- "Gregory worksAt Wazoo"

The agent can now reason that Ethan and Gregory both work at Wazoo, and Wazoo is
located in Seattle. It can infer that Ethan and Gregory share the same work
location.

## Item identifiers

In Worlds, subjects and objects are often identified by unique IDs (URIs). This
ensures that references to "Wazoo" always point to the same global entity in the
graph.

For a deeper dive into node identifiers, complex namespaces, and extending graph
topologies, check out our [Knowledge Graphs guide](/guides/knowledge-graphs).

### Summary

- **Subject**: The "who" or "what".
- **Predicate**: The relationship.
- **Object**: The target or value.
