In the previous module, you learned that a World is a graph. This lesson
explains how to build that graph.

## The triplet: The atomic unit of knowledge

Every piece of information in a World is stored as a **triplet**. A triplet
consists of three components:

1.  **Subject**: The entity you are describing (e.g., "Ethan").
2.  **Predicate**: The relationship or property (e.g., "worksAt").
3.  **Object**: The value or another entity related to the subject (e.g.,
    "Wazoo").

Together, they form a clear semantic statement: **Ethan works at Wazoo.**

### Graph formation

Triplets are powerful because they are universal. You can express everything
from simple facts to complex hierarchies through this structure.

As you add multiple triplets, they naturally form a **Graph**:

- "Ethan worksAt Wazoo"
- "Wazoo locatedIn Seattle"
- "Greg worksAt Wazoo"

The agent can now reason that Ethan and Greg both work at Wazoo, and Wazoo is
located in Seattle. It can infer that Ethan and Greg share the same work
location.

## Item identifiers

In Worlds, subjects and objects are often identified by unique IDs (URIs). This
ensures that references to "Wazoo" always point to the same global entity in the
graph.

### Summary

- **Subject**: The "who" or "what".
- **Predicate**: The relationship.
- **Object**: The target or value.
