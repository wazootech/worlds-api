Worlds combines traditional full-text search with vector-based retrieval to
provide a "Neuro-Symbolic" discovery experience.

## Search technologies

### Vector index (neural)

Worlds converts every piece of information into a high-dimensional vector to
enable **Semantic Search**. This allows agents to find information based on
meaning rather than exact keywords, providing the "intuition" for the system.

### FTS (symbolic)

Worlds uses **Full-Text Search (FTS)** for cases requiring exact matches (names,
terminologies, or codes). FTS ensures access to deterministic facts when the
exact query term is known.

### Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF)

The **Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF)** algorithm combines results from the Vector
Index and FTS. RRF ranks these results to provide the most relevant answer,
whether discovered semantically or via keyword.

## Chunks

Worlds handles large datasets by splitting knowledge into **Chunks**.

### The analogy: A page / fragment

Think of a Chunk as a **Page** in a book or a **Fragment** of a document. While
a Statement represents an "atom" of a fact, a Chunk provides a larger segment of
text for context.

### Technical equivalent: Document segment

Technically, a Chunk represents a **Document Segment** processed for indexing.
When Worlds imports a large document or conversation, it splits the content into
Chunks. Worlds then processes each Chunk in two ways:

1.  **Vectorization:** Enables semantic retrieval.
2.  **Linking:** Connects the Chunk to the Statements (triples) it contains,
    providing an audit trail from search results to specific facts.

Searching through Chunks allows agents to identify the relevant section of a
knowledge base and then drill down into specific Statements.
