Worlds doesn't just store data; it makes it discoverable. By combining
traditional full-text search with modern vector-based retrieval, Worlds provides
a "Neuro-Symbolic" search experience.

## Search Technologies

### Vector Index (Neural)

Every piece of information in a World is converted into a high-dimensional
vector. This allows for **Semantic Search**, where the agent can find
information based on _meaning_ rather than exact keywords. It's the "intuition"
part of the system.

### FTS (Symbolic)

For cases where exact matches matter (names, specific terminologies, codes),
Worlds uses **Full-Text Search (FTS)**. This ensures that rigid, deterministic
facts are always accessible when you know exactly what you're looking for.

### Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF)

To give you the best of both worlds, we use **RRF**. This algorithm combines the
results from both the Vector Index and FTS, ranking them to provide the most
relevant answer regardless of whether it was found semantically or via keyword.

## Chunks

Knowledge is often too large to be stored as a single "atom" or "world." This is
where **Chunks** come in.

### The Analogy: A Page / Fragment

Think of a Chunk as a **Page** in a book or a **Fragment** of a larger document.
While a Statement is an "atom" of a specific fact, a Chunk is a larger segment
of text that provides context.

### Technical Equivalent: Document Segment

Technically, a Chunk is a **Document Segment** that has been processed for
indexing. In Worlds, when you import a large document or a long conversation, it
is split into Chunks. Each Chunk is then:

1.  **Vectorized:** To enable semantic retrieval.
2.  **Linked:** Most Chunks are linked to the Statements (triples) they contain,
    providing an audit trail from a broad search result down to a specific
    atomic fact.

By searching through Chunks, your agent can find the relevant _section_ of a
knowledge base and then drill down into the specific _Statements_ that matter.
