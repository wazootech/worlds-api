Worlds is built on a foundation of professional, neuro-symbolic engineering and
local-first principles. Truly autonomous and reliable AI agents require a
persistent memory infrastructure that is as structured as it is accessible.

## Systemic foundations

Hybrid architectures bridge the gap between model reasoning and data ownership
by maintaining persistent memory and agent state.

### Hybrid architecture

Worlds uses a hybrid architecture to solve the reasoning gap in modern AI.

- **Probabilistic inference**: Generative AI extracts meaning from unstructured
  data.
- **Deterministic facts**: The queryable graph stores that meaning as facts you
  can verify. This ensures your agent navigates a structured map of reality
  rather than guessing context based on probabilities.

### Edge-first autonomy

An agent's memory must live close to the user. Worlds ensures that intelligence
and knowledge live on your local machine, a private server, or at the network
edge.

- **Local autonomy**: Agents never depend on a central cloud for their memory.
- **Low latency**: Local nodes retrieve memory significantly faster than global
  databases.

### The local-first manifesto

As agents become more autonomous, humans must retain ownership of the knowledge
those agents carry.

- **Residency**: Data lives close to you.
- **Ownership**: You own the triples; Worlds acts as the facilitator.
- **Portability**: The graph uses an open standard (RDF), preventing proprietary
  silos.

### Technical values

Foundational technical values guide the development of the World Engine.

### Bring your own brain (BYOB)

The Worlds infrastructure works with any intelligence layer. Whether you utilize
OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, or local models, the world acts as a detachable
hippocampus. You can swap model providers without losing your agent's persistent
state.

### Calm technology

experiences where the complexities of graph management, SPARQL optimization, and
vector synchronization are abstracted away. Use standard tools, and the engine
handles the heavy lifting such as Deno, libSQL, and edge network management.

### Small web and interoperability

Autonomy and data ownership are core to the platform's vision. By building on
permissive open-source standards (Apache 2.0) and utilizing protocols like MCP,
Worlds enables a decentralized ecosystem of interoperable knowledge graphs.

### Malleable knowledge

Data is not static. Knowledge must be as fluid as the environments it describes.
The Worlds architecture supports forking, merging, and mutating world models in
real-time.

A World acts as a **Digital Garden** for your agents—a private, evolving context
where relationships and history are maintained with deterministic integrity.
